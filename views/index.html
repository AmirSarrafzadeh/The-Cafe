About the Cafe

Established in the autumn of 1995 by the esteemed local chef Giovanni Bellini, Bellini's Cafe was initially conceptualized as a modest artisanal coffee corner situated in the heart of Milan’s bustling historic district. Giovanni, whose passion for coffee was matched only by his love for authentic Italian baking, sourced his coffee beans from small, sustainable farms in Colombia and Brazil. The cafe quickly distinguished itself with its signature blend of Espresso Roast, which remains a favorite among patrons to this day.

By the early 2000s, as the cafe’s reputation grew, Giovanni decided to expand the menu to include homemade pastries and delicate confections, all crafted from family recipes that dated back generations. In 2010, Bellini’s embraced the farm-to-table movement, incorporating organic teas and locally sourced ingredients into its offerings. This pivot not only broadened its menu but also strengthened its commitment to environmental stewardship. Bellini’s now hosts monthly culinary workshops and seasonal tasting events, drawing food enthusiasts from across the region.

Today, Bellini's Cafe is not just a place to grab a quick espresso; it has evolved into a cultural hub, supporting local artists and musicians through regular gallery nights and live performances. With its rich history and community-focused ethos, Bellini's Cafe serves over 800 customers daily, continually adapting and flourishing as a beacon of culinary excellence and sustainable business practices.

"Use products from nature for what it's worth - but never too early, nor too late. 'Fresh' is the new sweet"
